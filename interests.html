Camping and Hiking

I started going to summer camp in Ontario, Canada, when I was 9 years old. I quickly fell in love with nature and spent every summer there for the next 10 years. These summers were the most meaningful and formative summers of my life, challenging me in ways I didn't know was possible. When I was 17, I spent 8 weeks canoeing and portaging through northern Quebec, ending in an Inuit village on Hudson's Bay called Umiujaq. These experiences formed who I am today, you can watch a video on my trip right here: 2022 Hudsons Bay Trip

Hockey

I have been playing hockey for as long as I can remember. Both my parents played in college, so I was put into hockey skates as soon as I could walk. I was a left-wing my whole life and once I got into high school, I started playing defense. I played all four years of high school, and hockey will always be something that I stick with.

Graphic Design

I started taking graphic design classes in high school where I fell in love with creating and manipulating designs. I discovered my love for this when I started making merchandise for my school's sports teams. Now, I create the merchandise for my sorority Gamma Phi Beta here at UW-Madison.
